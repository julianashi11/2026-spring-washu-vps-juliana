Every night, or at least most, I call my dad. Whether that is complaining to him about a grade I got on a test or just simply telling him about my day, having someone to talk to at the end of the day calms a lot of those anxieties I hold in myself. Even though he doesn’t give the best advice at times, knowing that I have a person in my life who cares is what’s important to me. This ritual keeps me grounded in the craziness of school and makes me realize that there is so much more than just tests and assignments. It’s truly the people in my life who matter and that I should prioritize. At the end of the day, the people who are so important to me won’t be in my life forever. So, giving a call once a day to those I love is an extra moment that I can appreciate in my life. I believe that this teaches us about the importance of relationships. Having people that you can rely on is the root of what keeps humans sane. Thus, in the midst of exams and projects, I realize that giving a call to my dad is the best solution to calming my nerves.
