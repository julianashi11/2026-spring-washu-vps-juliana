A conversation about the storm

Kona
Low

An intimate account of the storm that stopped Hawaii — through the eyes of someone who has called the islands home for 35 years.

Read on
Question 01

When did you know this Kona Low was serious?

Answer

I started to notice that this storm was serious when our company was notified that work had been cancelled that day. In my 35 years living in Hawaiʻi, never once has work been cancelled due to a storm. This was the first in forever, and that was when I knew there was something truly wrong.

Question 02

If you had to describe the 'personality' of this specific storm — was it an angry guest or a cleansing force?

Answer

That is a complicated question that has a complicated answer. In the moment, it was definitely angry. It displaced many individuals on the North Shore and caused animals to fend for themselves. After the first wave, we thought the storm was gone — but when the second wave came, I truly believed there was some spirit that was angry at us. However, who knows… in the future the amount of rain could provide nourishment to our ʻāina, allowing our crops and plants to flourish.

Question 03

How did your household react to the storm?

Answer

Your dogs were frightened. We let them play in the yard every day, but on the day of the storm, they were shivering, waiting to be let back in the house. Even they knew something was wrong. Though we weren't impacted too badly, your mom, sister, and grandma still worried for those on the North Shore. We also had an outage which was annoying — but luckily we have a gas stove to cook food on.

Question 04

How did the ʻāina react to the storm?

Answer

The ʻāina was for sure not prepared for the immense amount of rain that would fall. The mountain ridges like Koʻolau could not handle the 10+ inches of rain that was impended upon the land.

Question 05

How did the community show the spirit of Kōkua (help) or Lokahi (unity) once the rain stopped?

Answer

After the storm, I saw people coming together as a community to clean neighborhoods and provide ʻohana to those who might have lost something. It was truly wonderful seeing people drive all the way to the North Shore just to help individuals who lost their homes — to clean the mud off their belongings.

Question 06

What is your reflection on this storm?

Answer

I believe that this storm taught us that in the bad, there always comes good. After the tragic storm, people came together as one ʻohana. No matter how bad something our island goes through, there will always be a helping hand. That is what I love about the aloha spirit our home embodies.

Question 07

How does it change your perspective on life?

Answer

This storm taught me to appreciate life a little more. As these storms can be life-threatening, I realized that moments spent with family and friends should be cherished.

Mahalo
