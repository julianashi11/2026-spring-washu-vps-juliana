This piece discusses the different phases of what technology has evolved to. In the present day, it is moving towards a term called ubiquitous computing, which is when many computers are shared among all of us. However, the challenge in shifting towards this concept is the idea of calmness and comfort. And to define the three signs of comfort: 1) easily moves from center to periphery and back, 2) enhances our peripheral reach 2) puts us at home. I noticed that it was interesting that the first two signs are more about how we view technology with our eyes, and the third one goes more into feelings and emotions. I liked how the first two resulted in the third sign. Going off the paper, I think a deeper message of this text is how we are becoming more and more interconnected with each other as time goes on. Since computers have the ability to connect us with people all around the world, those people who are 5,000 miles away can suddenly be right in front of our faces. In a way, this also brings a calming energy in that technology now allows us to remain connected with the people we love. Overall, I think this reading provokes a lot of deeper conversations and provides an inquisitive perspective on technology.
