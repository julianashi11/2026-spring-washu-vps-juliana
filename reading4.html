This article was able to give me a different perspective on webdesign and it’s purpose in our society. I truly enjoyed reading this text as it was able to connect websites to how it connects those around the world. One line that stood out to be was: “So when they ask me about actually making a website, they are referring to a website in the world … today.” The reason why I am highlighting this sentence is because it refers to the website as a timestamp. I interpreted it as like a piece of history that anybody can imprint into the internet. Furthermore, I also really enjoyed the personification of the website. Websites “allow the author to create not only works (the “objects”) but also the world (the rooms, the arrangement of roms, the architecture!).” I had always thought a website was only a website, but after reading this article, I realized that a website can truly be anything. So, I began to think about how I could make the websites that I am making into a metaphor. Specifically, I thought about how I wanted my restaurant website page to personify the environment of the website. I was inspired by its atmosphere and vibe: “Website as coffee chat.” When I came up with this, I wanted the website to be portrayed as a space where it offers that comforting feeling where people can connect over coffee and brunch. Overall, the impression that Schwulst’s piece was able to provide me impeccable insight into how I would like to imagine the impact I would like to leave with my website.
